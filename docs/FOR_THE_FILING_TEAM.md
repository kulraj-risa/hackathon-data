# A second pair of eyes before you hit "Submit"
### What we're building, in plain language — and what we'd love your feedback on

Hi — thanks for taking a few minutes. You file prior authorizations (PAs) every
day, so **you know this problem better than anyone.** We've built a tool to help,
and before we go further we want *your* gut check: does this match reality, and
would it actually help you?

---

## The problem we're trying to solve

Right now, roughly **4 out of every 10 pharmacy PAs get denied.** Most of those
denials aren't because the patient doesn't qualify — they're because **something
in the paperwork was missing or weak** when it went out the door:

- a prior medication the patient tried wasn't documented,
- a lab or test result was missing or too old,
- the quantity asked for was over the plan's limit,
- the diagnosis wasn't specific enough.

When that happens, the PA bounces back, the patient waits another week for their
medication, and you have to redo the work. **The frustrating part: a lot of those
denials were avoidable if someone had caught the gap before submitting.**

---

## What the tool does

Think of it like a **spell-checker for prior authorizations.** Just before you
submit, it looks at the case and tells you three things:

1. **A risk score** — "this PA looks like it has a high chance of being denied"
   (shown as a simple green / yellow / red dial).
2. **Why** — the specific things that are making it risky (e.g. *"no record of a
   tried-and-failed preferred drug"*).
3. **What to fix** — concrete suggestions, like *"add documentation of the prior
   therapy"* or *"attach a recent lab"* — so you can fix it **before** it goes out,
   not after it comes back denied.

You're still in charge. **It never submits or rejects anything on its own.** It
just gives you a heads-up, the same way a colleague glancing over your shoulder
might say, "Hey, did you attach the sleep study?"

---

## A quick example

> You're filing a PA for a brand-name drug with **Fidelis**.
> You hit "Check," and the dial turns **red — high risk.**
> It says:
> - ⚠️ *No documentation that the patient tried a preferred drug first.*
> - ⚠️ *Requested quantity is above the plan's usual limit.*
>
> You go back, add the note about the two drugs the patient already failed, and
> adjust the quantity. You hit "Check" again — now it's **yellow, then green.**
> You submit with confidence. **A denial just got prevented.**

---

## How does it "know" this?

We showed it **10,000 real past PA cases** — both approved and denied — and it
learned the patterns of what tends to get denied. So it's not guessing; it's
pattern-matching against thousands of real outcomes your team has already lived
through.

**It's not perfect, and we're not pretending it is.** It's a helper that catches
common, avoidable gaps. The final judgment is always yours.

---

## What it is — and what it is NOT

| It IS | It is NOT |
|---|---|
| A heads-up before you submit | A replacement for your judgment |
| A way to catch avoidable denials | An auto-approver or auto-denier |
| A list of *specific* things to fix | A black box that just says "no" |
| Built from your team's real cases | A tool that changes how you file |
